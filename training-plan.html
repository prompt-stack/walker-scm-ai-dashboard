The Three Cohorts

Cohort 1: Cabinet

~12 people Q1 2026

Who: C-Suite + VPs (budget holders)

Training: Strategic AI for Executives

  • Evolution of Intelligence
  • Traditional AI vs Generative AI
  • Large Language Models
  • Prompts & Prompt Engineering
  • Vendor Selection & Ecosystem

Outcome: Strategic alignment, budget decisions, sponsor AI initiatives

Cohort 2: Management (Non-Warehouse)

~15-20 people Q1 2026

Who: Directors + Managers in corporate functions, freight forwarding

Training: Foundations in AI + Workflow Discovery

  • Module 1: Evolution of Intelligence
  • Module 2: Traditional AI vs Generative AI
  • Module 3: Large Language Models
  • Module 4: Prompts & Prompt Engineering
  • + Department-Specific Workflows

Outcome: Submit FWI requests, map workflows, train their teams

Cohort 3: Management (Warehouse)

~10-15 people Q1 2026

Who: Regional Managers, Site Supervisors

Training: Foundations in AI + Workflow Discovery

  • Same 4-module foundation
  • Warehouse-specific use cases
  • Mobile-first considerations
  • Regional operations focus

Outcome: Submit FWI requests, map warehouse workflows, train floor teams

Location Consideration: Need to determine where warehouse managers are located (distributed across sites?) to plan in-person vs. virtual training.

Workflow Discovery Process

After training, managers facilitate workflow mapping with their teams.

Why Workflow Mapping Matters

The intake survey alone isn't enough. People report symptoms, not root causes.

  • Someone says: "I spend 2 hours on Excel every morning"
  • That's the symptom
  • The root cause might be upstream: two systems that don't talk, a process that shouldn't exist, etc.
  • If you just build an agent to automate the Excel shuffle, you've optimized a broken process

The Process

  1. Managers identify 3-5 key workflows in their area
  2. Document each workflow step-by-step (who does what, when, why)
  3. Identify friction points - where do things slow down, cause frustration, create errors?
  4. Prioritize by impact - which friction points, if solved, would save the most time?
  5. Submit to FWI - intake survey with context from workflow mapping

Operational Grammar

Teaching staff to evaluate their own workflows:

  • Breaking work down to micro-steps
  • Documenting processes before identifying AI opportunities
  • Understanding where AI fits in existing workflows
  • Not everyone has Six Sigma training - this fills that gap

Training Success Metrics

Leading Indicators (Track Immediately)

  • Attendance: Did all Cabinet/Management attend?
  • Engagement: Session feedback scores (1-10)
  • Comprehension: Can they explain key concepts?
  • Confidence: Self-reported comfort level before/after

Lagging Indicators (Track Q2+)

  • FWI Submissions: Are managers submitting intake requests?
  • Workflow Documentation: Have they mapped key processes?
  • Team Cascade: Are they training their direct reports?
  • Tool Adoption: Are they using AI tools weekly?

Q1 Goals

  • 100% of Cabinet trained (12 people)
  • 100% of Management trained (25-35 people)
  • At least 10 FWI intake submissions from management
  • At least 5 workflows documented per business unit
  • 1-2 AI Champions identified and given deeper training
