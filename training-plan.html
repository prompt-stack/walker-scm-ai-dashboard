Cohort Structure & Logistics

Cohort Size & Format

  • Cohort Size: 30 participants (optimal for interaction and engagement)
  • Session Length: 3 hours (half-day workshop)
  • Format: Hybrid (in-person preferred, virtual option available)
  • Frequency: 3 cohorts per week (Tue/Wed/Thu)
  • Follow-up: 2-hour advanced session 4 weeks later

Quarterly Training Roadmap

Q1 2026: Executive & First Functional Cohort (Jan-Mar)

Target: ~150 people trained

  • Week 1-2: Executive cohort (half-day intensive)
    • C-Suite and VPs (approximately 13 executives based on survey)
    • Focus: Strategic understanding, governance, cultural leadership
  • Week 3-4: Organizational diagnostic
    • Identify high-impact functional areas
    • Interview department heads
    • Map workflows and pain points
  • Week 5-12: First pilot cohort (one high-impact function)
    • 4 cohorts × 30 people = 120 people
    • Likely candidates: Business Development, Finance, or Operations leadership
    • Intensive training + 4-week pilot project
  • Week 13: Q1 review, refine approach

Deliverables: Governance framework, prompt library v1, pilot use cases identified

Q2 2026: Two More Functional Cohorts (Apr-Jun)

Target: ~360 people trained (cumulative: 510)

  • Week 1-13: Roll out to 2 additional functional areas
    • 12 cohorts × 30 people = 360 people
    • Function selection based on Q1 diagnostic
    • Examples: Operations, IT, Human Resources
  • Ongoing: Q1 cohort advanced training
    • 2-hour follow-up sessions
    • Share pilot learnings
    • Build function-specific prompt libraries
  • Focus groups: Monthly check-ins with each functional cohort

Deliverables: 3 functional prompt libraries, 3 pilot use cases documented

Q3 2026: Organization-Wide Rollout (Jul-Sep)

Target: ~360 people trained (cumulative: 870)

  • Week 1-13: Continue functional cohort training
    • 12 cohorts × 30 people = 360 people
    • Mix of remaining functional areas
    • Leverage internal champions from Q1-Q2
  • Trainer development:
    • Train 5-10 internal trainers from Q1-Q2 graduates
    • Begin co-facilitation model
  • Data security: Azure infrastructure deployment

Deliverables: 50%+ workforce trained, internal trainer program established

Q4 2026: Complete Rollout & Measure (Oct-Dec)

Target: ~330 people trained (total: 1,200)

  • Week 1-11: Final cohorts
    • 11 cohorts × 30 people = 330 people
    • All remaining knowledge workers
    • Internal trainers lead 50%+ of sessions
  • Week 12-13: Year-end assessment
    • Measure adoption rates across all cohorts
    • Document ROI from pilot projects
    • Survey participants for feedback
    • Plan 2027 advanced training

Deliverables: 100% knowledge workers trained, success metrics documented, 2027 roadmap

Training Curriculum

Session 1: Core AI Literacy (3 hours)

  • Hour 1: Foundations
    • What is AI? How does it work?
    • Walker SCM's AI strategy and vision
    • Tools overview: ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot
    • Governance and responsible use
  • Hour 2: Hands-On Practice
    • Writing effective prompts
    • Real Walker SCM use cases
    • Live exercises with your work
    • Common pitfalls and how to avoid them
  • Hour 3: Function-Specific Applications
    • Department-specific use cases
    • Workflow integration strategies
    • 30-day practice challenge
    • Q&A and troubleshooting

Session 2: Advanced Applications (2 hours, 4 weeks later)

  • Share successes and challenges from practice period
  • Advanced prompting techniques
  • Multi-tool workflows
  • Building your personal prompt library
  • Pilot project opportunities

Success Metrics & Tracking

Leading Indicators (Track Monthly)

  • Attendance: % of scheduled participants who attend
  • Engagement: Session feedback scores (1-10)
  • Practice: % using AI tools weekly (30 days post-training)
  • Confidence: Self-reported comfort level before/after

Lagging Indicators (Track Quarterly)

  • Adoption: % of trained workers using AI daily/weekly
  • Use Cases: # of documented success stories per function
  • Time Savings: Self-reported hours saved per week
  • ROI: Value generated from pilot projects

Risks & Mitigation Strategies

Risk: Low Attendance / Engagement

Mitigation:

  • Executive sponsorship and communication
  • Make training mandatory with calendar holds
  • Schedule during work hours (not lunch/after hours)
  • Incentivize attendance (e.g., certificate, recognition)

Risk: Training Doesn't Translate to Usage

Mitigation:

  • 30-day practice challenge with check-ins
  • Pair participants with "AI buddies" for accountability
  • Share success stories from early adopters
  • Make AI tools easily accessible (no IT friction)

Risk: Capacity Constraints (Can't Train Fast Enough)

Mitigation:

  • Develop internal trainers early (Q1-Q2)
  • Consider 4 cohorts/week in peak quarters
  • Offer virtual options to increase flexibility
  • Record sessions for on-demand learning

Risk: Uneven Quality Across Cohorts

Mitigation:

  • Standardized curriculum and materials
  • Train-the-trainer program for internal facilitators
  • Quality control through observation and feedback
  • Regular trainer calibration meetings
