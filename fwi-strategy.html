Frictionless Workforce Initiative (FWI)

Small, quickly deployed, AI-driven systems that make work easier and increase productivity. The FWI is about strategically using AI to take places where people are trying to achieve normal things but experiencing friction, and making it smooth.

"This is where we didn't just help them use AI to make us more beneficial at work, we help them use AI to become what they need to become, to work in the future world and to live in the future world."

Three Slices of the Pie

The FWI focuses on the first two slices. The third slice is a separate track for Q3-Q4 2026.

1. Training & AI Literacy

Focus: Q1-Q2 2026

  • Top-down education on AI tools
  • Using ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini out-of-the-box
  • Prompt engineering fundamentals
  • Skills that benefit work AND personal life

2. Custom Agentic Solutions

Focus: Q1-Q2 2026

  • Small, quick-deploy AI systems
  • 1-3 day builds only
  • Internal friction points
  • High ROI quick wins

3. Vendor AI Integration

Focus: Q3-Q4 2026

  • AI features in existing tools
  • Microsoft Copilot improvements
  • Evaluate vendor roadmaps
  • Leverage what's already paid for
Key Decision: Training and agentic solutions will run in parallel, not sequentially. We're not waiting until Q2 to start building agents - both tracks begin in Q1.

FWI Operating Rules

These six rules govern what qualifies for the Frictionless Workforce Initiative.

1 Internal Focus Only

Focus on internal systems and processes, not customer-facing systems. Customer-based AI opportunities go through regular IT project queue.

2 Scope/Cost/Time Limits

Quick hitters only. 1-3 days to build an agent. No 3-6 week projects under FWI. Larger projects go to regular IT queue.

3 Owner Documentation

Business owner who submitted the request documents usage and handles training for their team. Not IT's responsibility.

4 Owner Accountability

Submitter oversees productivity measurement collection. They report hours saved, who's using it, and user stories.

5 Limited Submission Rights

Only department heads or certain grade levels can submit requests. Provides natural screening before requests reach IT.

6 Centralized Prioritization

Kevin and his leadership prioritize all submissions. They decide what gets built and in what order.

Intake Survey Design

How friction points get submitted to the FWI.

MVP (January Launch)

  • Simple form: Name, Department, Problem description, Estimated hours spent/week
  • AI cleanup button: "Improve my description" (helps users articulate problems better)
  • Department head approval workflow

Future Enhancements (Q2+)

  • Voice input option (phone-optimized for warehouse supervisors)
  • AI follow-up questions (like deep research agents that ask clarifying questions)
  • Auto-categorization of requests
  • Integration with productivity dashboard

Design Principles

  • Computer AND phone friendly
  • Frictionless to submit (practice what we preach)
  • Not technical requirements - just "what's your pain point?"
  • Screening built in via department head approval
