AI Competency Matrix Assessment

Walker SCM Team Readiness Evaluation - November 2025

Overall Assessment: Pre-Beginner to Beginner

Non-Technical Team with Strong Foundation for Growth

Current Competency Levels

Scale: 1 = No Exposure, 2 = Pre-Beginner, 3 = Beginner, 4 = Intermediate, 5 = Advanced

Conceptual Understanding

Pre-Beginner to Beginner (2.5/5)

Understanding what AI is and how it works conceptually.

Evidence from Survey:
  • High comfort level (8.7/10) shows awareness and willingness to learn
  • Concerns about "reliability/accuracy" indicate conceptual gaps in understanding AI limitations
  • Limited tool variety (mostly ChatGPT/Copilot) suggests narrow understanding of AI ecosystem
  • Some respondents indicate "I don't know where to start"
Next Level:

Move to Beginner/Intermediate by understanding how different AI models work, their training data sources, and appropriate use cases.

Operational Skills

Beginner (2.5-3/5)

Knowing how to use AI tools effectively in daily work.

Evidence from Survey:
  • 35% daily users + 30% weekly users = 65% regular usage
  • Multiple use cases identified (admin, analytics, customer service)
  • Active informal experimentation across departments (70%)
  • Clear understanding that AI can save time on admin tasks
Next Level:

Move to Intermediate by exploring diverse AI tools, developing power-user skills, and establishing consistent workflows.

Governance & Ethics

Pre-Beginner (1.5-2/5)

Having systems and policies for responsible AI use.

Evidence from Survey:
  • 70% report "individual staff experimenting informally" - no organizational framework
  • Top concern: "Client data privacy and security" - awareness of risks exists
  • Interest in "Understanding AI ethics, data security and best practices" as priority
  • No formal governance structure mentioned in survey responses
Next Level:

Move to Beginner by establishing basic AI usage policies, data handling guidelines, and risk mitigation strategies.

Competency Level Definitions

Level 1: No Exposure

Haven't yet seen, handled, or managed any generative AI system. No experience across any swim lane.

Level 2: Pre-Beginner

Conceptual: Aware of AI, used it a couple times.
Operational: Occasional use, not sure how to maximize effectiveness.
Governance: No governance framework or policies in place.

Level 3: Beginner

Conceptual: Understand basic input/output mechanics, know AI can save time.
Operational: Active usage cadence (weekly/daily), multiple use cases, feel comfortable with technology.
Governance: Basic awareness of risks, starting to consider data privacy and accuracy concerns.

Level 4: Intermediate

Conceptual: Power user who understands how AI models work, can explain training data and generation process, familiar with several tools.
Operational: Daily usage across multiple tools, diverse use cases, can troubleshoot issues.
Governance: Established guidelines for appropriate use, data handling protocols, basic risk mitigation strategies.

Level 5: Advanced

Conceptual: Understand training data, non-deterministic nature of AI, limitations and capabilities across different models.
Operational: Established workflows and systems, built-in validation checks for outputs, optimization of prompts and processes.
Governance: Comprehensive AI usage policies, ethical frameworks, compliance procedures, regular audits and updates to governance structure.

Key Opportunities for Growth

  1. Conceptual Development: Provide education on AI fundamentals, model types, and appropriate use cases to build deeper understanding.
  2. Operational Excellence: Expand tool awareness beyond ChatGPT/Copilot, develop power-user skills, and create consistent workflows for high-value use cases.
  3. Governance Foundation: Establish clear AI usage policies, data handling guidelines, and risk mitigation strategies to move from informal experimentation to structured adoption.
  4. Leverage Informal Experimentation: Identify and scale successful pilot efforts happening across the organization (70% experimenting informally).
