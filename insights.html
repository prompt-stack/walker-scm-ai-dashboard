Key Insights & Findings

AI Readiness Survey Analysis - November 2025

AI Usage Frequency

AI usage frequency is directly tied to usefulness and exposure. Those who find AI more useful tend to use it more often, and usefulness is determined by exposure and understanding of how AI works within their workflow.

Key Finding: The more a person understands AI's capabilities, the more they integrate it into their job and personal use. Usage patterns reflect both comfort level and perceived value.

Department Distribution

The survey shows a concentration among executive leadership (52% of respondents). This executive engagement is critical for driving organizational AI adoption and setting strategic direction.

Key Finding: Strong executive participation indicates leadership buy-in, which is essential for successful AI implementation across the organization.

Age Range Analysis

Age range matters because different generations have varying feelings and aptitudes toward AI tools. Understanding the age distribution helps identify if particular age groups have specific concerns or adoption patterns.

Key Finding: Generational differences exist in the workplace, and tracking age-related patterns helps tailor training and support approaches for different demographic groups.

Comfort Level with AI Tools

Comfort level is crucial as it determines what kind of anxiety or psychological barriers a person might have when using generative AI tools. The average comfort level of 8.7/10 is encouraging.

Key Finding: High comfort levels suggest the team is ready to adopt AI solutions, with minimal psychological barriers to overcome. This readiness accelerates implementation timelines.

Top Concerns About AI

Understanding concerns reveals the anxieties people face. Key concerns include:

  • Reliability and accuracy of AI responses - Indicates need for education on risk mitigation
  • Client data privacy and security - Critical for building responsible AI practices
  • Cost or access to tools - Practical barrier to adoption
Key Finding: These concerns dictate the need for risk mitigation strategies around responsible AI use, accuracy validation, and clear guidelines on where AI is and isn't appropriate to limit organizational exposure.

60-Day AI Priorities

Priorities reveal what team members believe is important and where they want to focus efforts. Top priorities include administrative efficiency, data dashboards, and operational improvements.

Key Finding: Clear alignment on priorities (especially admin/reporting and data insights) provides a roadmap for immediate AI implementation opportunities with high perceived value.

Most Used AI Tools

Limited variety in tools used (primarily ChatGPT and Microsoft Copilot) indicates an opportunity for education about the broader AI ecosystem.

Key Finding: Knowledge of and experimentation with various AI tools correlates with enthusiasm for learning about generative AI. Expanding tool awareness can increase overall AI adoption and effectiveness.

Organizational AI Usage Patterns

This is a critical finding: The majority of AI use is happening through "individual staff experimenting informally." Combined with 35% daily users, this reveals:

Key Finding: Your organization has active AI users experimenting right now. These informal experiments and small pilots represent valuable learning opportunities. It's essential to unearth these efforts, learn from them, and potentially scale successful approaches across the organization.

The presence of "several pilots across units" and "small pilots in one area" indicates people are already trying to figure out what AI can do inside Walker SCM. This grassroots adoption is a strong foundation for formal AI strategy.

Key Problems AI Can Solve

Admin & Documentation emerged overwhelmingly as the top opportunity area, followed by operational efficiency, data analytics, and customer service.

Key Finding: The clear consensus on administrative and documentation challenges presents an immediate, high-impact opportunity for AI implementation. This is where quick wins can be achieved to build momentum and demonstrate value.

These findings provide a clear direction for AI strategy: focus initially on administrative efficiency while building capabilities for operational and analytical applications.
